Enlightment

The Enlightenment was a period of time when people developed new ideas about human existence, including peoples’ basic rights and the level of control they should have over their government and their futures. The Enlightenment began in Europe around 1715. People in cities like Paris gathered in salons to discuss philosophy and ways to improve the human experience. Many of their ideas were based in science and reason, which is why this time was also called the Age of Reason.

Renaissance

Renaissance Humanism was an intellectual movement typified by a revived interest in the classical world and studies which focussed not on religion but on what it is to be human. Its origins went back to 14th-century Italy and such authors as Petrarch (1304-1374) who searched out 'lost' ancient manuscripts. By the 15th century, humanism had spread across Europe.

Industrial Revolution

The Industrial Revolution refers to the greatly increased output of machine-made goods that began in England in the middle 1700s. Before the Industrial Revolution, people wove textiles by hand. Then, machines began to do this and other jobs. Soon the Industrial Revolution spread from England to Continental Europe and North America.

Reformation

This was a time in the History of Europe, when some people began to question some of the Teachings of The Catholic Church and to challenge the authority of the Pope. It began in Germany in 1517 as a protest against abuses in the Church. The supporters of this desire for reform were called “Protestants”.

Colonialism

The practice of a powerful country extending its control over another territory and its people, often involving political dominance, economic exploitation, and the forced movement of settlers to that land. Motivated by resource acquisition and economic gain, this practice was widespread from the 16th to the mid-20th centuries, with European nations establishing colonies across the globe.

Counter Reformation

The counter-reformation definition is a process by which the Catholic Church attempted internal reform and renewed evangelization. It has been called Counter-Reformation because it was a movement against, or counter to the Protestant Reformation.

French Revolution

The French Revolution was a watershed event in world history that began in 1789 and ended in the late 1790s with the ascent of Napoleon Bonaparte. During this period, French citizens radically altered their political landscape, uprooting centuries-old institutions such as the monarchy and the feudal system.

Mercantilism

Mercantilism, economic theory and practice common in Europe from the 16th to the 18th century that promoted governmental regulation of a nation’s economy for the purpose of augmenting state power at the expense of rival national powers. It was the economic counterpart of political absolutism.

Absolutism

The political doctrine and practice of unlimited centralized authority and absolute sovereignty, as vested especially in a monarch. The essence of an absolutist system is that the ruling power is not subject to regularized challenge or check by any other agency, be it judicial, legislative, religious, economic, or electoral.
